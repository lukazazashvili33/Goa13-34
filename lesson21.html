The development programme for the E60 began in 1997, concluding in 2002. The lead designer was Boyke Boyer.[6] The final design, developed by Davide Arcangeli[7] under BMW Design Director Chris Bangle,[8][4] was approved in 2000 and German design patents filed on 16 April 2002.[9]

The body of the E60 was made using aluminium for the front of the car, and steel for the passenger cabin and rear. Kerb weights are 1,445–1,725 kg (3,186–3,803 lb) for the sedan[13][14] and 1,675–1,830 kg (3,693–4,034 lb) for the wagon.[15][16] The weight distribution of the sedan models was 50:50.[17]
